What is SDG 4 · Target 4.5?
Introduction
The global goal
"By 2030, eliminate gender disparities in education and ensure equal access to all levels of education and vocational training for the vulnerable, including persons with disabilities, indigenous peoples and children in vulnerable situations." — United Nations, SDG 4, Target 4.5

Worldwide, over 240 million children live with a disability. Many face systematic barriers to accessing quality education — from physical inaccessibility of buildings to lack of trained teachers, adapted materials, or legal protections. SDG 4.5 challenges every country to close these gaps and build truly inclusive education systems.

Why France & Poland?
🇫🇷 France

France is often considered a model in Europe for inclusive education. The landmark 2005 Law on Equal Rights and Opportunities established the right of every child with a disability to be enrolled in their local mainstream school. Twenty years later, the results are significant — but challenges remain.

🇵🇱 Poland

Poland ratified the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities in 2012. Its education system offers families three paths: mainstream inclusive schooling, integration classrooms, or separate special schools. The transition toward full inclusion is still ongoing.

🇫🇷 France — Inclusive Education in Depth
Country Focus
A system built on the right to inclusion

Since the 2005 Disability Law, France has made inclusion in mainstream schools the default option. Every child with a disability has the right to be enrolled at their local school. The number of students included in mainstream settings has multiplied by 3.2 times since the law was passed — one of the most dramatic transformations in French education history.

Timeline of key measures
2005
Law of February 11, 2005 — Every child with a disability has the right to be enrolled in their local mainstream school. MDPH (departmental disability centers) created to assess needs and provide support.
2013
School Reform Act — Strengthens inclusive education; ULIS (Inclusive Units for Disabled Students) expanded in all school levels.
2019
Law for a School of Trust — Inclusive school principle enshrined in French law; AESH contracts standardized and strengthened.
2024
Pôles d'Appui à la Scolarité (PAS) — 100 new Support Hubs launched in 4 pilot departments to better coordinate school, medical, and social services for students with disabilities.
Remaining challenges
Undertrained teachers
The Cour des comptes (2024) highlighted that teachers still feel insufficiently trained to welcome students with disabilities in mainstream classes.
AESH shortage & low pay
Despite 140,000 AESH deployed, many students still lack full-time support. AESH positions remain precarious and poorly paid.
Sector coordination
The gap between the education system and the medico-social sector makes it difficult to provide consistent support across school and home environments.
Early school leaving
Students with disabilities are more likely to leave the education system early, particularly at secondary and post-secondary levels.

🇵🇱 Poland — Inclusive Education in Depth
Country Focus
A system in transition

Poland's education system offers three pathways for students with special educational needs: mainstream inclusive classes, integration classrooms (with a maximum of 5 students with disabilities among 20 pupils), or separate special schools. While families have the right to choose freely, many still opt for specialized institutions — particularly for children with more complex needs. The country is progressively moving toward inclusion, supported by European funding and national reform programs.

Timeline of key measures
1997
Education System Act — First legal framework allowing students with disabilities to attend mainstream schools; integration classrooms introduced.
2012
Ratification of the UN CRPD — Poland officially commits to inclusive education as a human right under international law.
2017
Education Reform — New regulations on early support and individual educational plans (IEPs) for students with special needs.
2023–2025
"School Accessible to All" Project (UNICEF + Ministry) — Targets training 11,200 teachers and supporting 30,000 students with disabilities in mainstream settings, funded through EU structural funds.
Teacher training gap
Most Polish teachers report feeling unprepared to teach students with complex learning needs in mainstream classrooms.
Segregated tradition
Special schools remain deeply embedded in the educational culture; some families prefer them, seeing them as providing more specialized care.
Geographic inequalities
Inclusive resources are concentrated in major cities; rural areas have very limited access to integration classrooms and specialist support.
Diagnosis delays
Late or inconsistent diagnosis of learning disabilities results in delays in receiving appropriate educational support.

Recommendations to reach SDG 4.5
📚
Train all teachers
Both countries must make inclusive education a core part of initial teacher training, not just optional workshops.
🤝
Connect school & health sectors
Better coordination between educational institutions and medical/social services ensures consistent support for students.
🌍
Share best practices
France and Poland can learn from each other — France's legal framework, Poland's reform momentum and EU-funded programs.
